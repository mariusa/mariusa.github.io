I solve business problems by using technology

I love making people happier and their businesses more effective by solving problems.

My professional background of 3 years of Product Management at Adobe, and 15 years of Software Engineering, together with a passion for User Experience Design, make me effective at this.

Let's improve your business! Here's how:

We'll go through these phases:

1. Tell me about your business problems (at no charge)

In several discussions, I'll learn about your business and what pains you and your staff are confronting. Usually, problems are about

  • spending too much time on a partially repetitive activity
  • high recurring costs
  • low quality outcomes

This phase is only about learning, identifying root causes, discussing workarounds you have tried etc.

Ideas about potential solutions are welcome, but we'll just collect and set them aside for later. As Sherlock Holmes put it best, "It is a capital mistake to theorize before one has data. Insensibly one begins to twist facts to suit theories, instead of theories to suit facts."

2. Design a solution

If things can be improved by using technology, I'll prototype a solution which solves your biggest problem, collaborating with a professional interaction designer.

We'll iterate over it until the proposed solution is clear, and you're happy with both the outcome and what it takes to start using it.

We'll also discuss costs of building and adopting this solution, compared with the problem's costs.

3. Build and deliver the agreed solution

Together with a team of talented software engineers, we'll build and deliver the fully functional solution, as discussed in the previous phase. We will integrate existing components and services wherever it makes sense, instead of reinventing the wheel.

We'll also help with training, legacy data imports or anything else required for a successful, gradual rollout to all staff which needs to start using it.

4. Monitor outcomes and make adjustments

After adopting the solution, we'll monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) we agreed to, and make adjustments to further improve them.
